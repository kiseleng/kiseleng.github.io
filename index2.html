Top Attractions in La Union Province

Tangadan Falls

While La Union is known for surfing, there are other things you can do in the province if you are not into this type of activity. La Union has plenty of waterfalls that you can visit, with Tangadan Falls as the most popular.

Baluarte Watch Tower

A 400-year-old watchtower facing the West Philippine Sea built during the spanish period, a 5.6-meter high made by reddish bricks structure located at Barangay Victoria, Municipality of Luna. Spaniards created the tower as a lookout point for the approaching attacks of Japanese, Chinese and Moro pirates and other conquerors in the coastal towns of la union. The guards were able to warn the locals of Luna to protect and defend their properties from ransacker. The baluarte also serves as a communication tower during world war II.

Namacpacan Church

Saint Catherine of Alexandria Parish Church, also known as the Shrine of Our Lady of Namacpacan and Namacpacan Church, is a Roman Catholic church located in Luna, La Union, Philippines under the jurisdiction of the Diocese of San Fernando de La Union

The Ultimate La Union Surf

La Union, known as the “Surfing Capital of the North,” is a must-visit destination for both surfing professionals and beginners alike. Located on the northern half of the region, San Juan is the main surfing hub, drawing in enthusiasts from all around the world. Just a four-hour drive from Manila, this coastal paradise offers pristine beaches that are perfect for catching waves and soaking up the sun.
